Regular Expressions

   

Because of their expressive power and extreme compactness, regular expressions (REs) have been applied in many diverse application areas of computer science and electrical engineering [Shaw79]. Recent advances in text indexing allow for text searching using regular expressions in logarithmic average time [Gonn89] independent of the size of the answer. Our work does not incorporate this indexing strategy because of the overhead involved, but such methods could be applied to make filtering based on REs very fast. We use REs to specify a pattern or a set of character strings to be matched in three filtering stages of an IS. We formulate REs for text according to the commonly accepted notation due to [Deut67], but we extend it slightly for alternation and specifying paragraph entities or combinations of REs on separate paragraphs within a message.

Definition: The RE formed by matches a message d if and only if and match separate, distinct paragraphs within d.

Definition (alternation): The RE formed by matches a message d if and only if or matches d.
