Thesis Outline

   

  The rest of this chapter describes ideas and research from the literature that are relevant to this work. We briefly introduce some important WAN and IR concepts  to build a basis for the central issues of our research. We conclude this chapter with a critical discussion of other SDI models that are outwardly similar to ours.

Chapter 2 defines some new models for information dissemination. We present a method for aperiodic  query  scheduling of WAN information sources based on their network  accessibility and the rate of change of information  in them. Then we propose improvements to some standard baseline IR indexing and retrieval methods. We go on to analyse the structure of WAN information items (messages) and finally present a simple method for incorporating this structure in a new term weighting function.

The third chapter introduces our concepts of WAN information filtering. We develop a user-model  based on the SDI and ``Agent''  designs (Agents are discussed below, in this chapter). We then introduce an information  system model which more formally defines these filtering stages , their purpose, motivation, and application. The information system model incorporates both the filtering stages and user-model. Finally, a new method of phrase generation for use in one filtering stage is presented.

The fourth chapter describes our subscription service (Pasadena ). We explain how user-profiles are realized, how queries are handled and the different methods of user  dialog. We then show how we implemented the query scheduling and filtering models introduced in chapters 2 and 3. We describe some of Pasadena's data  management and processing  methods and resource  management functions. Interesting implementation  aspects are discussed and we conclude with the results of numerous retrieval performance experiments  run in the system.

The final chapter starts with a discussion of the meaning and implications of the results  from the performance experiments. We then discuss the system's acceptance and usability, and conclude with some ideas for the future of WAN-based filtering.
