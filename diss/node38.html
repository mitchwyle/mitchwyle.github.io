System Usability

If the usual method [Giff90], [Lose91], [Scha90], [Malo87] of anecdotal user acceptance can be applied to appraise the usefulness of the Pasadena subscription service, then we can conclude that the system is an unqualified success. Many of the researchers and other individuals using the system found it extremely beneficial in their daily activities, not only to help them stay informed about current topics of interest, but also to find specific messages from the Matrix related to their work. Surprisingly sophisticated inclusion filtering stages were created to find very specific text patterns among the millions of WAN messages passing through the system. The model of an SDI service delivering messages via electronic mail also had a special appeal to certain individuals who prefer email to other media for its timeliness, flexibility, and ease of use.

However, the system was developed for the express purpose of evaluating the viability of the staged filtering model as well as testing and iteratively improving vector space retrieval methods. Therefore, the success or failure of Pasadena must be measured by the results of the experiments performed within the system. Because the usefulness and adjusted usefulness values in most of the evaluations are relatively low, the measured performance improvements (based on subscriber preference relations) in the algorithms tested are also small. We must reluctantly conclude, therefore, that although we have introduced significant improvements to existing algorithms, the amount of improvement is relatively small.
